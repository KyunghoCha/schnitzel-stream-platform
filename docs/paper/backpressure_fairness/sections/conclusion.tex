\section{Conclusion}
Under a frozen \(4 \times 4 \times 50\) protocol, native policy ranking is workload-dependent rather than uniform.
Significant differences are limited to \texttt{harm\_weighted\_cost} and \texttt{p95\_latency\_ms};
other tested metrics remain non-significant in this matrix.
The \texttt{error} policy results are fully reported as failed-run outcomes.

The submission artifact set is reproducible and contract-checked, with ROS2 comparison kept as appendix-only evidence.
Claims are restricted to measured comparisons in the fixed dataset and do not assert broader causal or universal behavior.
