\begin{abstract}
This paper reports a frozen-analysis benchmark of backpressure policy behavior under fairness-sensitive
stream stress. Native and ROS2 baseline systems each execute 800 runs
(4 policies \(\times\) 4 workloads \(\times\) 50 repeats) under a shared run schema.
In native results, the rank-1 policy differs by workload:
\texttt{weighted\_drop} (balanced, recovery\_stress),
\texttt{drop\_oldest} (minority\_spike), and
\texttt{drop\_new} (skewed\_burst).
Across 72 Holm-corrected pairwise tests among non-error policies, 10 are significant
and all significant findings are on \texttt{harm\_weighted\_cost} or \texttt{p95\_latency\_ms}.
The \texttt{error} policy is retained in reporting and shows zero successful runs in all workloads.
ROS2 results are treated as appendix evidence only; under this transport baseline, native means are lower
for harm and p95 latency in every comparable workload-policy cell.
All numeric claims are traceable to generated JSON/CSV artifacts.
\end{abstract}
